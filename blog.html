FoodBank — Mission, Vision & How It Works

Introduction

FoodBank is a community-driven platform created to reduce food waste and feed those in need. Our approach connects restaurants, events, and households with volunteers and local distribution points so surplus food is redirected quickly, safely, and respectfully.

Mission

To ensure surplus food reaches people, not landfills — focusing on speed, safety, dignity and community partnerships.

Vision

A society where surplus food is redirected to feed people, not wasted — creating stronger, more resilient communities.

How It Works (Step by step)

  1. Donor lists surplus: Restaurants or event managers list available food and pickup details.
  2. Volunteer accepts pickup: Volunteers or partner NGOs accept the listing and collect food following safety rules.
  3. Distribution: Food is delivered to shelters, community kitchens or directly to families in need.
  4. Confirmation: Donors and volunteers confirm delivery so records are kept for transparency.

Benefits & Use Cases

  • Reduces waste and landfill burden
  • Provides reliable food for shelters and vulnerable people
  • Helps restaurants reduce disposal cost and gain social impact
  • Fosters community involvement and volunteering

Safety & Requirements

Donors should package and label food, respect temperature handling and note allergens. Volunteers must follow basic hygiene and transport rules. We recommend packaging hot and cold separately and noting pickup windows accurately.

Call to Action

If you run a kitchen, consider listing surplus meals. If you can volunteer, sign up to help. If you need help, request it via the receive form — we’ll connect you to local donors.
